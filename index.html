Explore Jharkhand's Tribal Culture

Tribal Dance

Witness the energetic rhythms and vibrant costumes of Jharkhand's tribal dance forms, such as Chhau and Santhal. These dances celebrate harvest, festivals, and community spirit.

Tribal Art

Discover beautiful tribal art, including Sohrai and Paitkar paintings, intricate crafts, and ancient scripts carved on stones and wood, reflecting Jharkhand's rich heritage.

Tribal Festival

"Join the vibrant tribal festivals of Jharkhand, where locals gather to celebrate with traditional music, dance, and food. These festivals are a window into the state's ancient customs and joyful spirit."

Tribal Museum

The Tribal Museum in Ranchi showcases the rich culture of Jharkhand's 32 tribes. With detailed dioramas and photographs, it beautifully illustrates their traditional lifestyles, art, and pivotal role in the state's history

Eco Trails & Nature Walks

Forest Eco Trail

Trek through lush forests, listen to the sounds of nature, and spot diverse flora and fauna in Jharkhand's eco trails. Perfect for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

Hundru Falls

Experience the breathtaking beauty of Hundru Falls, one of Jharkhand's most famous waterfalls, surrounded by scenic landscapes ideal for hiking and photography.

Sunrise at Netarhat Hills

Experience a magical sunrise at Netarhat Hills, known as the 'Queen of Chotanagpur.' The rolling hills and morning mist create an unforgettable eco-adventure

Saranda Forest

Known as the "land of seven hundred hills," Saranda is the largest Sal forest in Asia, offering a peaceful haven with dense vegetation, curving roads ideal for cycling and hiking, and rich¬†biodiversity.
